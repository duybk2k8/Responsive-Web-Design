Ho Chi Minh

President Ho Chi Minh standing and greeting the Vietnamese people.

Here's a timeline of President Ho Chi Minh's life:

  • 1890 – Born on May 19 in Kim Lien Village, Nghe An Province, Vietnam, with the birth name Nguyen Sinh Cung.
  • 1901 – Changed his name to Nguyen Tat Thanh after the passing of his mother.
  • 1911 – Left Vietnam from Nha Rong Wharf aboard the Amiral Latouche-Tréville ship to begin a journey around the world in search of a path to national independence.
  • 1919 – Submitted the Eight Demands of the Vietnamese People to the Versailles Peace Conference, calling for freedom and equal rights for the Vietnamese people.
  • 1920 – Joined the Tours Congress in France and became one of the founding members of the French Communist Party.
  • 1923 – Traveled to the Soviet Union to study Marxism-Leninism and participate in international revolutionary activities.
  • 1925 – Founded the Vietnamese Revolutionary Youth League in Guangzhou, China, to train young Vietnamese revolutionaries.
  • 1930 – Presided over the conference that unified communist organizations, leading to the establishment of the Communist Party of Vietnam.
  • 1941 – Returned to Vietnam after more than 30 years abroad and founded the Viet Minh Front to unite the people in the struggle for independence.
  • 1945 – On September 2, read the Declaration of Independence at Ba Dinh Square in Hanoi, proclaiming the birth of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.
  • 1946 – Elected as the first President of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.
  • 1954 – Continued leading the country after the victory at Dien Bien Phu and the Geneva Accords, focusing on rebuilding the North and working toward national reunification.
  • 1960 – Became internationally recognized as a symbol of independence, peace, and freedom.
  • 1969 – Passed away on September 2 at the age of 79 in Hanoi.
  • 1987 – Honored by UNESCO as a Hero of National Liberation and a Great Man of Culture, recognizing his outstanding contributions to peace, national independence, and cultural development.
